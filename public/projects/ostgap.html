Outer Space Treaty Gap Analysis Project

A CGI research track on implementation gaps between the Outer Space Treaty and national space legislation.

What the project studies

OSTGAP examines how domestic space laws can implement, narrow, stretch, or leave ambiguity around obligations in the Outer Space Treaty. Its first phase focuses on non-appropriation, national authorization and supervision, and harmful contamination.

Methodology

The project compares treaty text, national implementation practice, and policy literature to identify areas where codification or interpretive clarification may reduce legal uncertainty.

Key questions

  1. How do national laws interact with Article II's non-appropriation rule?
  2. What does Article VI require for authorization and continuing supervision?
  3. How should Article IX obligations apply to environmental and operational risk?
Suggested citation

Celestial Governance Institute. (2026). Outer Space Treaty Gap Analysis Project final report. CGI.

Research scope

Article II

Non-appropriation

National implementation questions around resource activity, operational zones, settlement conduct, and claims of control.

Article VI

Authorization and supervision

Domestic licensing, continuing supervision, private activity, and responsibility for national space activities.

Article IX

Due regard and harmful interference

Consultation, contamination, environmental risk, proximity activity, and public-interest safeguards.
