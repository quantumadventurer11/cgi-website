Project overview

Treaty text, domestic implementation, and reform design.

OSTGAP evaluates where the Outer Space Treaty remains effective, ambiguous, underimplemented, dependent on state practice, insufficiently codified, or challenged by emerging activities.

The project has moved through multiple stages, including treaty-text analysis, national legislation analysis, expert consultation, and reform development. Its public materials emphasize Articles II, VI, and IX, while connecting those provisions to broader implementation and codification questions.

Why this matters

Treaty gaps become operational when activity diversifies.

Commercial activity

Private missions make authorization, continuing supervision, liability, registration, and national implementation central to treaty performance.

Emerging activities

Resource extraction, sustainability, settlement, and military activity test whether broad principles have enough operational detail.

Institutional coherence

Fragmented national approaches can increase legal uncertainty for states, operators, regulators, researchers, and affected publics.

Core analytical propositions

A codification-first reform orientation.

01

Many governance gaps arise from underimplementation rather than total absence of law.

02

National legislation is a major site of divergence in how treaty duties become operational rules.

03

Soft-law norms may require codification when recurring activity needs clearer standards.

04

Authorization and continuing supervision require clearer operational standards.

05

Resource activity and settlement expose unresolved questions in existing legal architecture.

06

Reform should distinguish treaty interpretation, implementation, supplementation, and replacement.

Project framework

From treaty provisions to institutional reform options.

Treaty-text analysis

Reviews the language, structure, and interpretive pressure points of the Outer Space Treaty and related instruments.

National implementation

Compares how domestic systems operationalize authorization, supervision, liability, registration, and private activity.

State practice

Identifies practice relevant to interpretation, implementation, and emerging norms without overstating consensus.

Expert consultation

Uses specialist review to test legal framing, reform pathways, and practical institutional implications.

Reform options

Distinguishes codification, interpretation, supplementation, and treaty replacement as separate pathways.

Institutional design

Examines whether legal duties need procedures, registries, review bodies, reporting systems, or model domestic rules.

Methodology and evidence standards

Legal research should make source authority visible.

OSTGAP uses doctrinal treaty analysis, comparative legal research, national-law analysis, state-practice review, expert consultation, source verification, and reform-option assessment.

Treaty text

Binding treaty provisions and interpretive context.

Binding national law

Domestic legislation, regulations, and licensing frameworks.

Official state practice

Public state positions, filings, practice, and implementation records.

Soft law

Guidelines, principles, standards, and non-binding instruments.

Scholarly interpretation

Academic analysis used to test and contextualize claims.

Institute analysis

CGI synthesis, gap classification, and reform-option assessment.
