Research preview

Golden Dome and space interceptor legal analysis.

A public-safe preview of CGI research questions on missile-defense architecture, kinetic and directed-energy interceptors, dual-use systems, and international space law.

Hero visual is a CGI-created concept illustration for research context. It is not an official Department of Defense architecture, operational diagram, or endorsement.

Status and boundaries

This page identifies an emerging CGI research track. It is not legal advice, not a finding that any system is lawful or unlawful, and not an adopted institutional position. Any formal output will require CGI review and sign-off before public release.

Research focus

The project treats Golden Dome as a case-study topic for legal analysis rather than as a policy endorsement or opposition statement. The work asks how space-based or space-linked missile-defense architectures should be characterized under existing international law and where legal uncertainty may require clarification.

Legal questions under review

  1. How Article IV applies to space-based military systems, weapons of mass destruction restrictions, conventional counterspace capabilities, and activity on celestial bodies.
  2. How Article III connects space activities to the UN Charter, the law on the use of force, self-defense, and other applicable international law.
  3. How Article IX duties of due regard, harmful interference consultation, and contamination avoidance relate to interceptor testing, deployment, debris creation, or reversible interference.
  4. How evidence, attribution, dual-use design, command-and-control, and proximity operations affect legal characterization.

Interceptor typology

Current research organization distinguishes kinetic hit-to-kill systems, directed-energy systems, dual-use and co-orbital capabilities, and offensive-overmatch risks. These are analytic categories for research discipline, not public conclusions about any specific architecture.

Official-source phrases under review

Method

Separate architecture claims from legal conclusions.

CGI will distinguish official public statements, proposed architectures, industry and contract materials, technical capability categories, treaty text, state practice, soft-law instruments, and original legal analysis. Public pages will summarize only what is appropriate for release.

Kinetic

Hit-to-kill systems

Legal questions include debris, target characterization, testing, proportionality, and harmful interference risks.

Energy-based

Directed energy

Research questions include reversibility, dazzling or damage thresholds, attribution, and escalation risk.

Dual-use

Co-orbital capabilities

Rendezvous, servicing, inspection, and proximity operations can blur benign and counterspace functions.

Offensive overmatch

The project will assess how defensive framing, operational design, and strategic effects may diverge.
